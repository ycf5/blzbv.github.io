13. Evaluating PCA Results

Evaluating PCA Results

Below are a series of quizzes about evaluating the results of PCA. Good luck!

When evaluating how many components to include, a good rule of thumb for the total amount of variance to be captured by the kept components would be…
SOLUTION: 80%

Question 2

So now that you have built out the process for the other three climate effects, how many components might best represent the amount of variance that we want in our model for precipitation?
SOLUTION: Three or four components

Question 3

On the scree plot for the rain effect, at what point would you say is the “elbow” in the plot that indicated how many components to keep?
SOLUTION: 3-4

Reflect

QUESTION:

Could there be an argument for not using all four climate effects? Explain your response.

ANSWER:

Yes, because precipitation could be considered redundant since it typically is the combination of rain and snow.

We would also want to apply the principal components process to the following variables: Longitude, Latitude, LastWinterFreezeDiff, and FirstWinterFreezeDiff.
SOLUTION: False
