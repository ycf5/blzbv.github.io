12. Model Selection Assessment

Intro

Methodology Map Assessment

Now that we’ve gone through several scenarios, let’s get some more practice determining the right methodology for a variety of business problems. For each of the following descriptions, select the correct methodology. Use the methodology map as a guide to help make the decision.

Methodology Map

Q10

A large bank is contemplating changing operating hours at all of its bank branches. They want to use a data-driven way to estimate the impact of changing the operating hours before rolling out the change to all its branches.
SOLUTION: A/B Testing

Q1

A high-end home goods manufacture wants to determine which customers in their mailing list are most likely to make a purchase if a catalog was sent to them.

SOLUTION: Binary

Q2

That same high-end home goods manufacture now wants to determine how much revenue they can expect if they send a catalog to their entire mailing list.

SOLUTION: Continuous

Q3

A pet store chain has 13 stores and would like to expand and open a 14th store. In order to determine the best location, the company wants to predict the sales it could expect from each potential location.

SOLUTION: Continuous

Q5

A small bank suddenly has an influx of new people applying for loans. It wants to determine which loan applicants are creditworthy by predicting whether or not each applicant is likely to default on their loan.
SOLUTION: Binary

Q6

An upscale coffee chain wants to know whether a television campaign about their recent introduction of gourmet-sandwiches will provide a good return on investment.

SOLUTION: A/B Testing

Non-predictive

A restaurant chain wants to see how much revenue each of its locations produced last year.
SOLUTION: Non-Predictive

Q7

A company that creates and sells video games wants to forecast monthly sales data in order to synchronize supply with demand, aid in decision making that will help build a competitive infrastructure and measure company performance.

SOLUTION: Time Based

Q8

A friend of yours is looking to find a good deal on a diamond for his soon to be fiancé. He has a large dataset of diamonds and wants to predict what the price of each diamond should be based on its physical attributes.
SOLUTION: Continuous

Q9

A university is concerned about dropouts. They want to be able to predict whether a student is likely to drop out so they can provide helpful interventions to help them succeed.
SOLUTION: Binary

Q11

A large grocery chain wants to understand whether a change from a link to an image on its website will lead to more mobile app downloads.

SOLUTION: A/B Testing

Q12

A mountain resort wants to predict the number of monthly room bookings it will have next year to help decide whether it should expand.
SOLUTION: Time Based
